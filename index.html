Frequently Asked Questions

What does a tree surgeon actually do?

Our tree surgeons are hands-on professionals skilled in trimming, pruning, felling, and sometimes planting trees. Every day brings different challenges - from assessing risks and checking habitats to safely removing problematic branches. It's not just about chainsaws; we conduct thorough risk assessments, ensure wildlife protection, and provide solutions that balance safety with environmental care.

How can I tell if a tree needs removing?

Look for warning signs like leaning trunks, mushrooms at the base, or cracks in major limbs. A sparse canopy or early yellowing leaves can also indicate health issues. Some problems are obvious, while others like internal rot may be invisible. It's always better to have a professional assessment than risk unexpected damage during storms.

Do I need permission to remove a tree?

Sometimes, yes! Trees in conservation areas or those with Tree Preservation Orders require council approval before any work. We always check local regulations and can liaise with council officers on your behalf. It's also courteous to discuss plans with neighbours if roots extend into their property.

Is stump grinding really necessary after tree removal?

Leftover stumps often become trip hazards, eyesores, or homes for pests and fungi. Grinding eliminates regrowth, clears the site completely, and gives your garden a fresh start. Unless you want ongoing maintenance issues, we recommend proper stump removal.

How do I choose a trustworthy tree surgeon?

Look for professionals with public liability insurance, NPTC/Lantra certifications, and genuine customer reviews. Real experts provide risk assessments, clear quotes, and reliable service. Avoid operators offering suspiciously cheap deals - quality tree care requires proper training and equipment.
